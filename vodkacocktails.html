Vodka Cocktails

  1. Vodka Martini

    2. The ultimate classic drink, favorites of men such as James Bond, and all those who desire to emulate him.

    In the words of Jed Bartlet: Shaken, not stirred, will get you cold water with a dash of (gin) and dry vermouth.

    The reason you stir it with a special spoon is so not to chip the ice. James is ordering a weak martini and being snooty about it.

    Vermouth is a tricky addition to the martini, at least for those uninitiated in how to order a martini correctly.

    1. "Dry" refers to the addition of only a splash of vermouth to lend a bitter taste to the drink.
    2. "Extra Dry" will only have a tiny drizzle of vermouth, or none at all.
    3. "Wet" martinis will have close to equal parts of vodka and vermouth.
    4. A "Perfect" martini will have equal parts of dry and sweet vermouth mixed with the vodka.

    Ultimately the vodka martini is garnished with 3 olives on a toothpick. Blue Cheese stuffed olives are a favorite.

    The addition of olive juice to the traditional martini will create the "dirty" variation.

  2. Moscow Mule

    3. The Mule is a simple yet classic cocktail often served with Brunch. The spicy tastes given by the Ginger Beer help it be good cut the sweet tastes of most sweet brunches.

    Simply mix 2 parts vodka and 4 parts spicy Ginger Beer and add a splash or two of fresh lime juice. Serve over ice in a copper mug and voila.

  3. Bloody Mary

    4. The Bloody Mary is a staple of brunches across the world, but perhaps it is served and respected the most in Wisconsin.

    The various restaurants and bars of Milwaukee have made a name for themselves as having some of the best recipses in the world.

    The simplest of recipses are 1 part vodka to 2 parts tomato juice with some type of salt, spices, and garnish.

    Salt or Celery Salt are the most common additions, but the spices and garnish almost never stop there.

    Worcestershire sauce, celery, horshradish, olives, and cheese are common additions to provide more flavor to the Bloody.

  4. Carmel Appletini

    5. Honestly a late addition to this list. My mother-in-law is recently retired to a beach in Florida, and has recently taken a liking to sweet cocktails.

    Yes. You read that correclty. Not sweet as in gnarly, sick, or awesome. Rather, sweet as in sugary. Her other favorite is the Dirty Girl Scout - who knows what's in that.

    Anyways, she was visiting and mentioned her new favorite drink. So logically I had to add it to this list.

    To be honest, I think the name needs some work, you'll get me once you read the ingredients.

    Start with a classic martini glass and drizzle Caramel inside the glass for both taste and asthetics.

    Take a cocktail shaker and pour in 2 ounces of the vodka of your choice (Absolut is her preference),

    add 2 ounces of sour apple pucker and then 2 ounces butterscoth schnappes and a healthy amount of ice. Shake well and pour straight into the glass, no additional ice.

    Optional garnishes include a Maraschino Cherry for looks, Apple slices as a garnish, and pure cane sugar for taste.
